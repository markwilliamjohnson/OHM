Octopus Recursion Simulator

This model separates recursion, coordination, and environmental knowledge. High recursion increases local autonomy within and across arms; coordination determines whether those semi-independent actions integrate coherently; environmental knowledge determines how well the organism identifies opportunities and moves toward food.

With high recursion, the arms now probe and wiggle much more independently like exploratory octopus limbs. With low recursion, movement is tighter, more uniform, and more centrally restricted.

What the controls do

Recursion depth: increases local autonomy and the variety of arm-level exploratory behaviour.
Coordination quality: determines whether autonomous limbs integrate well or fragment into conflicting actions.
Environmental knowledge: affects sensing radius, food detection, and directed movement toward opportunity.
Challenge: increases hazard load and makes low-adaptability regimes more difficult to sustain.

High recursion with poor coordination now harms viability through fragmentation. Low recursion with high coordination can still remain reasonably viable through tighter, centrally coherent control.
