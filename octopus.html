Octopus Recursion Simulator

This app treats the octopus as a distributed intelligence: recursive organization lets different environmental challenges be handled autonomously by local components that still work coherently together.

More recursion means more adaptive autonomy across the body. Less recursion means more rigid top-down management and lower adaptability, but death should come only after prolonged inability to manage complexity.

What the slider does

Food attractors: local recursive components can identify opportunities and coordinate movement toward them.
Hazards: trigger local avoidance, but recursion allows many distributed responses to integrate seamlessly.
Current field: recursive layers let posture and sensing adapt fluidly to changing flow.

High recursion increases distributed autonomy and adaptability. Low recursion still permits survival more often than before, but it produces reduced adaptability, slower recovery, and greater chronic strain under sustained challenge.
