Company Organization & Autonomy Lab

This simulator treats the octopus as an organization. Each arm is a semi-autonomous workstream made up of local cells that sense, interpret, and act.

Recursion depth now represents the extent to which intelligence is distributed across nested levels inside each arm. Higher recursion means stronger local self-governance, more adaptability, and more autonomy when coupling is good. Lower recursion means more central command-and-control, with less discretion and less capacity to adapt locally.

How the arms behave

Blue glow: recursive intelligence distributed through the arm. With good coupling, higher recursion increases local autonomy without losing alignment.
Green tint: adaptive coordination. Local cells act independently but remain mutually informative and coherent.
Red tint: fragility. This appears when complexity outruns autonomy, or when cells are badly coupled and cannot coordinate their local decisions.

In this version, higher recursion is not more bureaucracy. It is deeper distributed organization inside the arm. If coupling is strong, the arm becomes both more autonomous and more adaptive. If recursion is low, control collapses back toward the center and local cells lose discretion.
