Deregulation Institution Simulator

This interface turns the “10-to-1 deregulatory agenda” into an inspectable institutional model. It separates rhetorical force from causal mechanism, then runs Monte Carlo simulations to estimate likely distributions of public-interest gain, monopoly risk, patient risk, and discourse distortion.

Move the sliders to explore how different assumptions change the policy story. The key question is not whether the rhetoric sounds attractive, but whether the underlying mechanisms actually support its conclusions.

Institutional parameters

0 = absent / weak, 100 = dominant / strong. Simulations recalculate live when you move any slider.
The model uses stochastic variation around each parameter so the output is a distribution rather than a single deterministic score.

What the model is testing

Speech-side mechanisms

  • Regulatory burden falls disproportionately on small firms and independent providers.
  • Large incumbents can absorb or shape regulation more easily.
  • Removing “bad” regulation improves innovation, competition, and patient-first care.

Critical-side mechanisms

  • Paperwork may encode accountability, safety, traceability, and equity.
  • Deregulation can shift burden to patients, publics, and weaker providers.
  • Anti-bureaucratic rhetoric can hide evidence gaps, capture risks, and externalized harms.
