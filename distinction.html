Distinction-Making Lab

This app treats a distinction not as a thing, but as a relationship between an observer and what gets included or excluded. A box or circle is therefore not just an object on the screen: it is a way an observer marks a difference between what is inside and what is outside.

When you create multiple distinctions, you are not only observing individual distinctions, but also observing the relationships between distinctions. Those relationships can start as simple lines, then be reinterpreted as processes of attenuation and amplification.

Reading the diagram

Observer: the distinction exists as a relationship between the observer and a marked difference.
Boundary: not a thing in itself, but a mark that separates inside from outside.
Inside: what the distinction includes or privileges.
Outside: what the distinction excludes, backgrounds, or treats as environment.
Attenuation/Amplification: every relation between distinctions can be read as filtering some differences and enlarging others.
