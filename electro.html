Flux and induction

Changing magnetic flux creates emf.
This graph shows magnetic flux through a coil and the induced emf from its rate of change. A faster change means a larger induced voltage.

Electric field

The electric field tells us the force per unit charge. A positive charge is pushed in the direction of the field, while a negative charge is pushed the other way.

Felectric = qE

Magnetic field

A magnetic field acts on moving charge. The magnetic force is always perpendicular to the velocity, so it bends motion rather than simply speeding it up or slowing it down.

Fmagnetic = q(v × B)

Electromagnetic induction

When magnetic flux through a loop changes, a voltage is induced. This is one of the central ways electric and magnetic phenomena are linked.

emf = −N dΦ/dt

What you are seeing

In this view, the particle starts with a chosen velocity. The electric field pushes it left or right, while the magnetic field bends its path. A stronger magnetic field produces tighter curvature; a stronger electric field adds more direct acceleration.

This is a teaching model rather than a full physics engine with SI units. The aim is to make the relations visible and intuitive while keeping the controls responsive.
